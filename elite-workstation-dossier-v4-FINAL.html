Market Context · Why Prices Aren't Coming Down

This isn't a supply-chain blip. It's a global AI arms race.

Every hyperscaler on earth — AWS, Microsoft, Google, Meta, xAI, Oracle, Anthropic — is buying every HBM wafer Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron can produce. Every other week a new datacenter or AI warehouse breaks ground (including right here in the Carolinas). HBM3e consumes ~3× the wafer capacity per bit that consumer DDR5 does — and the fabs are fixed. Samsung, Hynix, and Micron have all shifted allocation away from consumer DRAM and NAND toward enterprise AI memory.

Fab capacity can't magically expand. A new leading-edge fab takes 18–24 months to build and another 12 months to ramp. We don't have the infrastructure, we don't have the trained technicians, we don't have the tooling. DDR5 spot prices are up ~307% since September 2025. NAND contract pricing is up 33–40% quarter-over-quarter. Micron exited consumer SSDs entirely in February 2026. Samsung has quietly discontinued entire SATA SKUs. Analysts at TrendForce are modeling elevated prices through 2028.

Translation for a buyer: the tower in front of you costs $3,700+ to rebuild today, and that figure is going up, not down. Every month you wait to buy a machine like this, the replacement cost climbs. This is why I'm firm at $2,300 — and why it's still a deal.
